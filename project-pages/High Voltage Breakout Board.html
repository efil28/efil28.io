High Voltage Breakout Board (QBMET)

I led the High Voltage sub-team for the Queen's Biomedical Engineering Team (QBMET) to develop a custom power distribution interface for a robotic exoskeleton. My primary responsibility was ensuring a reliable, high-density power link between the main 24V battery source and the motor controllers. I managed the full hardware lifecycle, from initial requirement gathering with mechanical sub-teams to final physical assembly and testing.

Engineering Leadership & System Architecture

As the technical lead, I translated the exoskeleton’s high-torque requirements into a robust electrical architecture. I coordinated cross-functional design constraints to ensure the power rail could handle the rigorous demands of robotic movement.

  • Design Ownership (Altium Designer): I spearheaded the full-cycle development of the PCB, specifically engineering the stack-up to include 2oz copper weight. By applying IPC-2221 standards, I optimized trace widths to handle continuous 23A loads, effectively mitigating thermal risks during peak operation.
  • System Protection & Reliability: I conducted trade-off analyses to integrate automotive-grade protection, specifically selecting TVS diodes to neutralize inductive kickback—a critical failure mode in high-torque robotic actuators.
  • Fabrication & Validation: I personally executed the SMD and through-hole assembly for the prototypes. Post-fabrication, I led a validation phase involving thermal imaging and stress-testing to ensure zero voltage sag and reliable current delivery under maximum mechanical load.

Results & Impact

The final board successfully powered the QBMET testing rig, maintaining a stable 24V output even during 23A peak surges. By designing a custom solution in Altium Designer rather than utilizing off-the-shelf components, we reduced the total power-system footprint by 40%, significantly improving the overall ergonomics and weight distribution of the exoskeleton.
