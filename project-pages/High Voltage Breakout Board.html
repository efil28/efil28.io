High Voltage Breakout Board

Developing a robust 24V power distribution interface for the Queen's Biomedical Engineering Team (QBMET) exoskeleton.

Design Objectives & Requirements

The primary goal of this project was to facilitate safe and efficient power delivery between the main 24V battery source and the motor controllers of the exoskeleton. As a critical point of failure, the board needed to meet rigorous safety standards while maintaining a high current throughput.

  • Current Handling: Designed to support continuous 30A loads with peak surge protection.
  • Thermal Management: Utilized 2oz copper weight and thermal vias to dissipate heat during high-torque motor operations.
  • Reliability: Integrated fuse protection for individual subsystems to prevent total system failure in the event of a localized short.

Results & Future Iterations

The final prototype was successfully integrated into the QBMET testing rig. During initial bench testing, the board maintained stable voltage regulation under simulated load conditions.

Future iterations of this design will focus on integrating real-time current sensing via an I2C interface to allow for telemetry logging during exoskeleton operation. This data will be vital for optimizing battery life and identifying mechanical inefficiencies in the limb joints.
