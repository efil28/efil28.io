High Voltage Breakout Board

Developing a robust 24V power distribution interface for the Queen's Biomedical Engineering Team (QBMET) exoskeleton. Designed for high-current reliability and user safety.

Project Overview & Technical Specs

The Challenge: The QBMET exoskeleton requires a stable 24V rail to power high-torque motors while maintaining a compact form factor. The goal was to create a breakout board capable of handling significant current surges without thermal failure or signal interference.
Technical Implementation:
  • Integrated heavy copper traces to minimize $I^2R$ power losses and heat generation.
  • Implemented transient voltage suppression (TVS) diodes to protect sensitive logic from inductive kickback.
  • Designed the PCB layout in Altium Designer, focusing on optimal component placement for thermal dissipation.
